Stress

We get stressed.So do animals.Shortage of food water conflict with their neighbours, infections, predators, temperature fluctuations can all cause them stress.Even humans and human activities.Stressed animals suffer just as much as humans do.Can we find out the animals that are under stress and help reduce it?Animals can show a wide spectrum of behaviour when they are under stress.But they cannot communicate with us directly.It is also extremely difficult to follow them and study their behaviour under natural conditions, without disturbing them.But there are other ways to monitor and even measure stress.You must have noticed that when we are stressed,our bodies get warm,we sweat and our heart beats increase.That happens because the glands in our bodies produce glucocorticoid hormones.These are steroid molecules.They are secreted into our blood stream,and help us face difficult situations.This happens also in animals.So,presence of steroids in blood can,this,be used as an indicator of stress.This is the strategy Dr Umapathy and his team use at the Laboratory for conservation of Endangered species in the Centre for cellular and Molecular Biology.This is great,but there is still one problem.Generally,blood samples are used .But getting blood samples from wild animals is challenging for researchers,and stressful for the animals.So,Dr Umapathy's team has devised an alternative.
